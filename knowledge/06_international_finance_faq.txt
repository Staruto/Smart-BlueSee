[International Students - Visa & Permits]
International Office:
  - Location: International Building (also referred to as Portland Building 215 for some services).
  - Tel: 8818 0188.
  - Email: internationaloffice@nottingham.edu.cn
  - Hours: Mon-Fri 09:00-17:00.
Visa (X1/X2):
  - X1 visa: For studies longer than 180 days. Must convert to Residence Permit within 30 days of arrival.
  - X2 visa: For studies 180 days or less. No residence permit needed.
  - Apply at Chinese embassy/consulate in your home country before travel.
Residence Permit:
  - Must apply within 30 days of arrival in China.
  - Documents: Passport, JW202 form, admission letter, health check certificate, passport photos.
  - Cost: ~400-800 RMB depending on duration.
  - Renewal: Apply 30 days before expiry at the International Office. They will guide you through the process.
  - Health Check: Required at the Ningbo Entry-Exit Inspection and Quarantine Bureau. The International Office arranges group visits.
Important: Overstaying your visa/residence permit results in fines (500 RMB/day, max 10,000 RMB) and possible detention. Always renew on time.

[International Students - Daily Life]
Bank Account:
  - Bank of China has a branch on campus (near the dormitory area).
  - Required documents: Passport, admission letter/student ID.
  - Most students open a Bank of China debit card for daily use.
  - Alipay and WeChat Pay can be linked to international bank cards or the BOC card.
Mobile Phone & SIM:
  - China Mobile and China Unicom SIM cards available at campus convenience store or nearby shops.
  - Bring your passport for registration (real-name registration required by Chinese law).
  - Recommended: China Mobile campus plan (~30-50 RMB/month with data).
  - WeChat is essential for daily communication in China.
Shopping:
  - Yinzhou Wanda Plaza: Large shopping mall, 15 min by bus. Cinemas, restaurants, clothing stores.
  - Tianyi Square: Main commercial district of Ningbo, 40 min by bus.
  - Online shopping: Taobao/Tmall, JD.com, Pinduoduo. Delivery directly to campus.
  - Campus convenience stores carry daily essentials.
Healthcare:
  - Campus Clinic for basic care (Room 109, Building 23).
  - For serious illness: Ningbo Yinzhou No. 2 Hospital or Ningbo First Hospital.
  - Insurance: International students should have comprehensive health insurance. Check with the International Office.
Cultural Integration:
  - Buddy Program: Pairs international students with Chinese student buddies.
  - Chinese language courses available through the Language Centre (free for enrolled students).
  - Cultural excursions organized each semester (Hangzhou, Shanghai, local Ningbo sites).
  - International Festival (Spring semester) — showcase your country's culture.

[Exchange Programs & Study Abroad]
Incoming Exchange:
  - UNNC welcomes exchange students from partner universities worldwide.
  - Apply through your home university's exchange program.
  - Duration: Typically 1 semester or 1 academic year.
Outgoing Exchange (for UNNC students):
  - Exchange opportunities with 50+ partner universities globally.
  - Destinations include: University of Nottingham (UK), KTH (Sweden), NUS (Singapore), University of Melbourne (Australia), and many more.
  - Application period: Usually October-November for the following academic year.
  - Requirements: Minimum 2:1 average (60%+), good academic standing, language requirements vary by destination.
  - Contact: Exchange Office in the International Building or exchange@nottingham.edu.cn.
Summer Programs:
  - Short-term summer schools at partner universities (2-6 weeks).
  - Some carry academic credits.
  - Financial support: UNNC Global Mobility Scholarship available for eligible students.

[Scholarships & Financial Aid]
Entry Scholarships:
  - Provost's Scholarship: Covers full tuition. For outstanding Gaokao/international qualification results. Highly competitive.
  - Vice-Chancellor's Scholarship: Covers 50% tuition.
  - Faculty Scholarships: 25-50% tuition. Faculty-specific criteria.
  - International Excellence Scholarship: For non-Chinese international students.
Continuing Scholarships:
  - Dean's Scholarship: Awarded to top-performing students at end of each year. Up to 100% tuition.
  - Academic Excellence Award: Cash prize for students achieving First-class marks.
  - Sports Scholarship: For students representing the university in competitions.
Other Financial Support:
  - Hardship Fund: Emergency financial assistance for students facing unexpected difficulties. Apply at The Hub.
  - Government Student Loans: Chinese national students may apply through the national student loan system.
  - Part-time Work: Students can work on campus (library, SU, events). Maximum 20 hours/week during term.
  - Note: International students need to check their visa conditions regarding work permissions.
Application: Most scholarships are automatically considered based on academic results. Some require separate application — check UNNC website.

[FAQ - Student ID Card]
Q: How do I get my student ID card?
A: New students receive their ID card during registration in Welcome Week. Lost cards: report to The Hub, replacement 30 RMB.

[FAQ - eduroam Wi-Fi]
Q: How do I connect to eduroam Wi-Fi?
A: SSID: eduroam, Username: yourid@nottingham.edu.cn, Password: your university password. Issues? Contact IT Desk (Portland 305, 8818 0009).

[FAQ - Exam Results]
Q: Where can I check my exam results?
A: Blue Castle (bluecastle.nottingham.ac.uk). Results released ~2-3 weeks after exam period.

[FAQ - Transcript Application]
Q: How do I apply for a transcript?
A: Apply through The Hub (Portland 120). Cost: 20 RMB/copy. Processing: 5 working days.

[FAQ - Missed Exam / Extenuating Circumstances]
Q: I missed an exam due to illness. What do I do?
A: Submit an Extenuating Circumstances (EC) claim through your School Office within 5 working days. Include medical evidence (doctor's note from the Campus Clinic or hospital).

[FAQ - Module Changes]
Q: How do I change my modules after enrollment?
A: Contact your School Office within the first 2 weeks of teaching. After that, changes are generally not permitted.

[FAQ - Program Transfer]
Q: Can I transfer between programs?
A: Program transfers are possible in limited circumstances. Discuss with your Personal Tutor and School Office first, then apply through The Hub.

[FAQ - Dormitory Maintenance]
Q: Where do I report maintenance issues in my dorm?
A: Contact the dormitory management office or use the campus maintenance app. For urgent issues (flooding, no power), also call 8818 0000.

[FAQ - Gym & Sports Access]
Q: How do I access the gym and sports facilities?
A: Present your student ID at the Sports Centre reception. Gym membership: ~80 RMB/semester. Swimming: 10 RMB/session.

[FAQ - Holidays]
Q: When is the next holiday?
A: Check the Academic Calendar section. Major holidays: Chinese New Year (Jan/Feb), Qingming (Apr), Labor Day (May 1-5), National Day (Oct 1-7).

[FAQ - Student Clubs]
Q: How can I join a student club?
A: Check the Student Union website or attend Freshers' Fair at the start of semester. Most clubs accept members year-round.

[FAQ - Stress & Counseling]
Q: I'm feeling stressed/anxious. Where can I get help?
A: Campus Counseling Center (Portland Building 118, 8818 0168). Free, confidential. For crisis support: 8818 0000 (24h).

[FAQ - Transport to City Center]
Q: How do I get to Ningbo city center from campus?
A: Weekend shuttle bus to Tianyi Square, or take public bus/metro from near the South Gate. Didi (ride-hailing) is also convenient.

[FAQ - 2+2 Program]
Q: What is the 2+2 program?
A: Study Years 1-2 at UNNC, then transfer to the UK campus for Years 3-4. Same degree awarded. Apply through your School Office in Year 1.
