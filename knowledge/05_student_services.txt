[Mental Health & Wellbeing Services]
Campus Counseling Center:
  - Location: Portland Building, Room 118.
  - Tel: 8818 0168.
  - Email: counseling@nottingham.edu.cn
  - Hours: Monday - Friday, 09:00 - 17:00.
  - Services: Individual counseling (free, confidential), group therapy, workshops on stress management, mindfulness, anxiety.
  - Appointments: Book via email or walk-in during office hours. Typical wait: 1-3 working days.
  - Languages: Counseling available in English and Chinese (Mandarin).
  - All counseling is strictly confidential unless there is risk of harm.
Crisis Support:
  - 24-hour Emergency Hotline: 8818 0000 (campus — routes to security/crisis team).
  - National Mental Health Hotline (China): 400-161-9995 (24h, free, Chinese/English).
  - Beijing Suicide Research & Prevention Center: 010-8295-1332 (24h).
  - Crisis Text Line: Text "HOME" to 741741 (international, English).
Peer Support:
  - Student Wellbeing Ambassadors: Trained student volunteers. Contact via Student Union.
  - Mental Health First Aiders: Selected staff trained to provide initial support. List available at The Hub.
Self-help Resources:
  - Togetherall: Free 24/7 online mental health community. Register with your university email.
  - SilverCloud: Online CBT programs for anxiety, depression, stress. Access via the Counseling Center.
  - Headspace: Free subscription for all UNNC students. Sign up with your university email.
Wellbeing Events: Wellbeing Week (November), Mental Health Awareness Month (May), De-stress events during exam period.
Important: If you or someone you know is in crisis or feels unsafe, call 8818 0000 immediately. You are not alone.

[Disability & Accessibility Support]
Disability Support Team: Portland Building, Room 116.
  - Email: disabilitysupport@nottingham.edu.cn
  - Tel: 8818 0156.
Services:
  - Reasonable adjustments for exams (extra time, separate room, scribe, reader).
  - Note-taking assistance.
  - Accessible accommodation arrangements.
  - Assistive technology (screen readers, speech-to-text, magnification).
  - Study skills support for students with specific learning difficulties (e.g., dyslexia).
Registration: Students must register with the Disability Support Team and provide relevant documentation (medical certificates, educational psychologist reports).
Buildings: Most buildings have step-free access and lifts. Accessible toilets are available in all main buildings.

[Accommodation & Housing]
On-campus Dormitories:
  - Available for Year 1 and international students (priority). Upper-year students may also apply.
  - Room types: Single room with shared bathroom (most common), ensuite single room (limited).
  - Facilities: Air conditioning, hot water, shared kitchen (each floor), laundry room, communal lounge.
  - Cost: Approximately 5,000-8,000 RMB per semester depending on room type.
  - Moving in: Typically 1-2 days before Welcome Week. Key collection at the dormitory reception.
  - Rules: No smoking indoors, quiet hours 23:00-07:00, no unauthorized guests overnight.
  - Maintenance: Report issues to the dormitory management office or through the campus maintenance app.
Off-campus Housing:
  - Many upper-year students choose to live off-campus.
  - Apartments near campus: typically 1,500-3,000 RMB/month for shared apartments.
  - Popular areas: Yinzhou Wanda, Gaoxin District, areas around the South Gate.
  - Register your off-campus address with the local police station (required by Chinese law for all residents).
  - The university does not manage off-campus housing but The Hub can provide guidance.
