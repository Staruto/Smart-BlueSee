[Course Enrollment & Module Selection]
Platform: NottinghamHub (hub.nottingham.ac.uk)
Enrollment Window: Typically opens 1-2 weeks before each semester starts. Check Moodle announcements.
Steps:
  1. Log in to NottinghamHub with university credentials.
  2. Navigate to "Module Enrollment" section.
  3. Select your core (compulsory) and optional modules.
  4. Submit your choices before the deadline.
  5. Wait for confirmation email from your School Office.
Module Changes: You can add/drop modules within the first 2 weeks of teaching. After that, contact your School Office.
Core vs Optional: Core modules are mandatory for your program. Optional modules let you personalize your degree.
Module Clashes: If two modules have overlapping lecture/exam times, you cannot take both. Check timetables before enrolling.
Prerequisites: Some modules require you to have passed specific earlier modules. Check module specifications.

[Examinations & Assessment]
Exam Timetable: Published on Moodle approximately 4 weeks before the exam period. Check regularly.
Exam Venues: Mainly in the Gymnasium and large lecture halls. Assigned seat numbers are published with the timetable.
What to bring: Student ID card (mandatory for identity check), approved stationery. Calculators must be on the approved list.
Prohibited items: Mobile phones, smartwatches, unauthorized notes, programmable calculators.
Duration: Most exams are 2-3 hours. Some modules have 1-hour computer-based exams.
Extenuating Circumstances (EC): If illness or personal issues affect your exam performance, submit an EC claim through your School Office with supporting evidence (e.g., doctor's note) within 5 working days.
Resit Exams: If you fail a module, you may be offered a resit opportunity (typically in August). Resit results are capped at 40%.
Viewing Results: Blue Castle (bluecastle.nottingham.ac.uk). Results typically released 2-3 weeks after each exam period, subject to Exam Board confirmation.

[Moodle - Learning Management System]
URL: moodle.nottingham.ac.uk
Login: University username and password.
Features:
  - Module pages: Lecture slides, readings, tutorial sheets, assessments, deadlines.
  - Announcements: Lecturers post important messages here. Enable email notifications.
  - Assignment Submission: Upload coursework through Turnitin links on Moodle. Check file format and size requirements.
  - Grades: View module grades and feedback (once released by marker).
  - Discussion Forums: Ask questions and interact with classmates and lecturers.
  - Recorded Lectures: Some modules offer lecture recordings via Panopto (embedded in Moodle).
Tip: Bookmark your Moodle pages. Check it daily for updates.
Mobile App: Moodle has a mobile app for iOS and Android. Search "Moodle" in your app store and use the UNNC Moodle URL to connect.

[Personal Tutor System]
Every student is assigned a Personal Tutor upon enrollment.
Role of Personal Tutor:
  - Academic support: discuss academic progress, module choices, study strategies.
  - Pastoral care: first point of contact for personal difficulties affecting studies.
  - References: Personal Tutors write academic references for job applications, postgraduate study, etc.
  - Signposting: direct you to specialist services (counseling, disability support, careers).
Meetings: At least 3 scheduled meetings per academic year. You can request additional meetings.
Change of Tutor: If you wish to change your Personal Tutor, contact your School Office with a reason.
Tip: Build a good relationship with your Personal Tutor early. They are your main academic advocate.

[Academic Appeals & Complaints]
Academic Appeals: If you believe your exam results are incorrect due to procedural error, you may submit an academic appeal.
  - First: discuss informally with your module convenor or School Office.
  - Formal appeal: submit through The Hub within 10 working days of results release.
  - Grounds: procedural irregularity, circumstances not previously considered, prejudice/bias.
  - NOT grounds: disagreement with academic judgment alone.
Student Complaints: For non-academic issues (facilities, services, staff conduct):
  - Stage 1: Informal resolution — discuss with the relevant department.
  - Stage 2: Formal complaint — submit in writing to The Hub.
  - Stage 3: Review by the Provost's Office.
Ombudsperson: If internal processes are exhausted, students can contact the UK Office of the Independent Adjudicator (OIA).
